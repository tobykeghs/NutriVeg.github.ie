A low-carb diet reduces carbohydrates — such as those found in grains, starchy vegetables and fruit — and involves foods high in protein and fat. Many types of low-carb diets exist, each diet has varying restrictions on the amounts of carbohydrates you can eat.

Purpose

A low-carb diet is mostly used for losing weight. Some low-carb diets may have health benefits rather than just weight loss, such as low risk factors associated with type 2 diabetes and metabolic syndrome.

Why you might follow a low-carb diet

You might choose to that on a low-carb diet because you:
Want a diet with low carbs to help you lose weight
changing your overall eating habits
Enjoy the types and amounts of foods featured in low-carb diets

Health Benefit (weight Loss)

Most people can shed weight if they reduce the number of calories consumed and increase physical activity levels. To lose 1 to 1.5 pounds (0.5 to 0.7 kilogram) a week, you need to cut down your daily calories by 500 to 750 calories. Cutting calories and carbs may not be the only reason for the weight loss. Some studies show that you may shed some weight because the extra protein and fat intake which helps in keeping you full longer, in return helps you eat less.
