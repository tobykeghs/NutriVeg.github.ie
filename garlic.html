A garlic allergy occurs when your body’s immune system mistakenly identifies garlic as being harmful and produces antibodies in an attempt to fight it. This reaction can takes place immediately upon contact, or within two hours after eating or touching garlic. One can also have an adverse reaction to garlic without being allergic to it. This is known as a food intolerance, and is more common. A food intolerance to garlic can lead to indigestion, heartburn, or gas. A food intolerance is not caused by the immune system. The symptoms are less serious.

Symptoms

Garlic allergy symptoms can range from mild to serious. They include:

  • Skin inflammation
  • hives
  • nasal congestion or runny nose
  • itchy nose
  • sneezing

Complications

Someone allergic to garlic can trigger their by breathing little amount of dust from either dried garlic or garlic skin, you may experience an allergy-induced asthma attack. Asthma symptoms are shortness of breath and wheezing. Asthma attacks can escalate quickly and become very serious and should not be taken lightly. An allergist can work with you on strategies for limiting your exposure to garlic. They can also recommend medications for asthma, which can help with symptoms. Another potential complication from a garlic allergy is anaphylaxis. Anaphylaxis is a severe, potentially fatal allergic reaction. Anaphylaxis caused by garlic allergy is rare. It’s more likely to occur from exposure to raw, rather than cooked, garlic.
