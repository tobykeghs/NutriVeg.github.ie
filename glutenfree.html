A gluten-free diet excludes the protein gluten. Gluten are found in grains such as wheat, barley, rye, and a cross between wheat and rye called triticale. A gluten-free diet is used in managing signs and symptoms of celiac disease and other medical conditions related to gluten. A gluten-free diet is, however, is also for people without any medical issues. The benefits of the diet are improved health, weight loss and increased energy. Removing gluten from your diet likely to reduce overall intake of fiber, vitamins and other nutrients. Therefore, regardless of the reasons for following a gluten-free diet, it's important to monitor your overall nutritional needs. For those just getting started on a gluten free diet, the task can be very overwhelming. Identifying what to and what not to avoid is where most people get stuck or frustrated.

1. Grains

As part of the gluten free “Golden Rule”, grains should not be eaten to prevent reactions to gluten. These items contain bread, pasta, cereals, muffins, bagels, cookies, and cakes.

2. Condiments and Seasonings

The following foods should be from from the diet unless it is stated on the label that they are gluten-free: Worcestershire sauce, MSG, modified food starch, malt products, bouillon, barley malt, and soy sauce. Also be aware that many gravies and salad dressings are thickened with gluten containing flours and grains.
