Meat, poultry, fish, beans and peas, eggs, nuts and seeds supply many nutrients and are an important part of healthy eating. these healthy foods contain not just protein but also, B vitamins (niacin, thiamin, riboflavin, B6 and B12), vitamin E, magnesium, zinc and iron. Salmon or tuna, almonds and walnuts provide some wonderful nutrients. The healthy proteins in meat, beans and nuts help in building bones, muscles, cartilage, skin and blood. They help in production of enzymes and hormones. B vitamins found in this food group help the body to release energy and build tissue. Vitamin B12 helps in having healthy blood, mostly found onlyin animal products. vegetarian can eat food like eggs, milk or cheese or take supplement. Nuts and seeds are great sources for fatty acids and vitamin E. Beans are source of fiber.

Types of protein

  • Enzymes:Enzymes build and break down molecules. They are important for growth, digestion, and other processes in the cell.
  • Motor Protein:Motor proteins helps cells moving and changing shape. They also aid transporting components around inside cells.
  • Storage Protein:Storage proteins helps in storing nutrients and energy-rich molecules to use later.

Carbohydrates are your body’s most important source of energy: They helps in fueling your brain, kidneys, heart, muscles, and central nervous system. For example fiber is a carbohydrate that helps in digestion makes you feel full and keeps blood cholesterol levels low. Your body can store extra carbohydrates in your muscles and liver to make use of when you dont eat so much carbohydrate or you are on a low carb diet.

Types of Carbuhydrates

  • Sugar: They are molecules of simple sugars like glucose, fructose (fruit sugar), and galactose, they are known as monosaccharides.
  • Starch:Starches are polysaccharides or "complex carbohydrates," made up of long chains of glucose. that your body breaks down starches fast than others into glucose to produce energy.
  • Fibre:Fiber is a carbohydrate found in cellulose of plant-based foods like grains, fruit, vegetables, nuts, and legumes.

Fat gets a bad reputation even though it is a nutrient that we need in our diet, but in little quantity. Learn all about dietary fats and how getting too much or too little affects our health. Dietary fats are important to give your body energy and to support cell growth. They also help in protecting your organs and to keep your body warm. Fats aids your body absorb some nutrients and produce important hormones, meaing your body definitely needs fat.

Types of fat

  • Saturated fats: A fat that contains just saturated fatty acids, is turns solid at room temperature, and comes chiefly from animal food.
  • Trans fats:An unhealthy substance that is produced through the chemical process of hydrogenation of oils.
  • Monounsaturated fats:Monounsaturated fat is a type of dietary fat. It is one of the healthy fats, monounsaturated fats are liquid when placed in room temperature, but harden when chilled. ... Monounsaturated fats are mostly found in plant foods, such as nuts, avocados, and vegetable oils.
  • Polyunsaturated fats:Polyunsaturated fat is a dietary fat. It is a of the healthy fats, along with monounsaturated fat. Eating controlled amounts of polyunsaturated (and monounsaturated) fat instead of saturated and trans fats can be benefitial your health
