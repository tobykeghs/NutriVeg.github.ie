Many very low-calorie diets are commercially-made formulas of 800 calories or fewer that replace all the food you usually eat. Others, such as the well-known grapefruit diet rely on eating a lot of the same low-calorie food or foods. Very low-calorie diets are not the same as over-the-counter meal replacements, which you substitute for one or two meals a day.

How Effective Are Very Low-Calorie Diets?

If you have a BMI over 30 , then a very low-calorie diet may help you lose about 3 to 5 pounds per week, for an average total weight loss of 44 pounds over 12 weeks. Losing that amount of weight improves weight-related medical conditions, which including diabetes, high blood pressure, and high cholesterol.

One of the most difficult part of weight loss is cutting down on calories. Many low-calorie foods can one feel hungry and unfulfilled between meals, making it tempting to overeat. Fortunately, there are foods that are both filling and low in calories.

What Makes a Food Filling?

High volume

When foods contain a lot of water or air, the volume is increased without adding calories and helps in filling you up

High protein

Studies show protein is a better option and filling compared to carbs and fat. Diets higher in protein increase satiety and lead to lower overall calorie intake than lower-protein diets do.

High fiber

Fiber helps provide bulk and helps you feel full. It also aid in slowing the movement of food through your digestive tract, which keeps you feeling fuller for longer.
